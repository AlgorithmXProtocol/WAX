How Algorithm X Works

The Algorithm X contract offers privacy in transactions on the Polygon network. It uses a unique ghost account generation algorithm and balance splitting in transfers to protect transaction confidentiality and user privacy.

When a user makes a transaction with Algorithm X, the contract automatically generates a ghost account, which acts as an intermediate account that hides the actual sending and receiving addresses on the public blockchain. Then, the balance is split into multiple addresses and sent in fractional amounts to these addresses, making it difficult to trace the exact amount and sending/receiving address of the transactions.

Furthermore, the Algorithm X contract uses advanced encryption and obfuscation techniques to protect transaction data and ensure the security of user information, adding an extra layer of privacy. This helps maintain the privacy of transactions and protect the identity of users involved in Algorithm X transactions on the Polygon network.

It's important to note that Algorithm X has undergone thorough auditing to ensure its security and reliability, providing users with peace of mind when using the contract. However, as with any blockchain project, it's essential for users to take additional precautions and properly safeguard their private keys and data to maintain the security of their transactions and digital assets.
